Bitcoin

Bitcoin is a decentralized digital currency created in 2009 that uses peer-to-peer technology to facilitate instant payments. It follows the ideas set out in a whitepaper by the mysterious Satoshi Nakamoto, whose true identity remains unverified.

History

Bitcoin's inception traces back to 2008 when a whitepaper titled "Bitcoin: A Peer-to-Peer Electronic Cash System" was published under the pseudonym Satoshi Nakamoto. The first Bitcoin transaction occurred in 2009, marking the beginning of a new era in digital finance.

Technology

Blockchain

The blockchain is a public, distributed ledger that records all Bitcoin transactions. It's structured as a chain of blocks, each containing a group of transactions and a hash of the previous block, ensuring the integrity and immutability of the entire chain.

Blockchain Visualization

Mining

Bitcoin mining is the process of adding transaction records to the public ledger (blockchain) and introducing new bitcoins into the system. Miners use powerful computers to solve complex mathematical problems, securing the network and processing transactions.

Economics

Bitcoin operates as a decentralized digital asset, designed to function as a medium of exchange. Its unique economic properties, including a capped supply of 21 million coins, have led some to view it as "digital gold" and a potential hedge against inflation.

Bitcoin Market Capitalization

Global Impact

Bitcoin has had a profound impact on the global financial landscape, challenging traditional banking systems and sparking debates about the future of money. Its adoption varies widely across different countries, with some embracing it and others imposing restrictions.
